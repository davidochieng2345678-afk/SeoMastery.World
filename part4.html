How to Get Brand Mentions – The 5-Step System

Getting brand mentions is a systematic process. Here's the 5-step system that has helped 100+ Kenyan businesses get mentioned in trusted sources:

1 Identify Target Sources

Create a list of podcasts, media outlets, blogs, and communities in your industry. Focus on sources that trusted by AI systems and relevant to your niche.

2 Create a Compelling Pitch

Develop a unique angle or story that journalists, podcast hosts, and bloggers want to cover. Data, case studies, and unique insights get the most attention.

3 Pitch Consistently

Send 5-10 pitches per week. Follow up after 3-5 days. Consistency is key — most mentions come from persistent, professional pitching.

4 Amplify Your Mentions

Share every mention on social media, your website, and email newsletters. Amplification signals to AI systems that your brand is worth talking about.

5 Build Relationships

Relationships > Transactions. Nurture relationships with journalists, podcast hosts, and influencers. Repeat mentions come from strong relationships.

How to Create an llms.txt File – Your AI-Friendly Roadmap

llms.txt is the new robots.txt for AI. It tells AI systems which pages to cite and how to understand your content structure. Here's how to create one:

📄 llms.txt Template for Kenyan Businesses

# llms.txt – AI-Friendly Roadmap for [Your Business Name]
# Created: [Date]
# Last Updated: [Date]

# Main Website
https://[yourwebsite].com

# Core Content Sections
https://[yourwebsite].com/about
https://[yourwebsite].com/services
https://[yourwebsite].com/blog
https://[yourwebsite].com/contact

# Key Service Pages
https://[yourwebsite].com/services/service-1
https://[yourwebsite].com/services/service-2
https://[yourwebsite].com/services/service-3

# Essential Blog Posts
https://[yourwebsite].com/blog/post-1
https://[yourwebsite].com/blog/post-2
https://[yourwebsite].com/blog/post-3

# Resources
https://[yourwebsite].com/resources/guide-1
https://[yourwebsite].com/resources/guide-2

Save this as llms.txt in your website's root directory (e.g., yourwebsite.com/llms.txt)
